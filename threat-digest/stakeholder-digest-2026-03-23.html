Notable campaigns & headlines

Themes that matter for briefings and risk committees:

  • Supply chain & CI/CD: Compromise of the Trivy scanner ecosystem — infostealer via GitHub Actions, reported worm and destructive Kubernetes-related activity. Reinforces controls on third-party actions, signing, and pipeline secrets.
  • Cloud & AI abuse surface: Research and reporting on attack paths in AWS Bedrock; abuse of Azure Monitor alert channels for callback phishing. Worth a joint cloud + security architecture review.
  • Phishing at scale: IRS-themed campaigns (~29k users cited), Signal/WhatsApp targeting (FBI warning), and continued innovation in social engineering.
  • Endpoint & browser: “VoidStealer” and related reporting on stealing Chrome secrets via debugging interfaces — relevant to EDR policy and browser hardening.
  • Operational noise: Exchange Online change impacting mail access — distinguish from malicious activity in SOC playbooks.

Recommended actions

  1. Patch governance: Run a same-day inventory pass on SharePoint, Cisco FMC/SCC, Craft CMS, Livewire apps, Zimbra, Wing FTP, and Apple fleet components; align to KEV due dates.
  2. Pipeline security: Review GitHub Actions allow-lists, OIDC usage, and artifact integrity; incident-check any use of Trivy or similar scanners in CI.
  3. Cloud controls: Short workshop on Bedrock IAM, logging, and guardrails; review Azure Monitor alert templates and user education on callback phishing.
  4. Phishing response: Refresh user reporting paths; ensure IRS / messaging-app lures are in current awareness content.
  5. Stakeholder loop: Share this one-pager with IT ops, cloud owners, and incident response; reconvene in one week on closed KEV items and any new CISA additions.
